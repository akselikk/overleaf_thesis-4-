\subsection{Fuel Polynomial}
\label{app:notation:polynomial}

The fuel consumption rate $f_{ij,v}$ is computed from the SCA vessel
polynomial:
\begin{equation}
  f_{ij,v} = \sum_{k=0}^{6} a_k^{(\ell)} \cdot v^k,
  \label{eq:fuel_poly_app}
\end{equation}
where the coefficients $a_k^{(\ell)}$ depend on the loading condition
$\ell \in \{\text{light, medium, full}\}$ and are provided by SCA Logistics.
The polynomial is valid for $v \in [10, 16]$~kn and exhibits a
local non-monotonicity at 12--13~kn in the light-load regime.

For arcs where the mean cargo weight $w_{ij}$ does not match a single
loading condition, the model interpolates between adjacent polynomial
coefficients using linear blending on cargo fraction:
\begin{equation}
  f_{ij,v} = (1-\lambda) \cdot f_v^{(\ell)} + \lambda \cdot f_v^{(\ell+1)},
  \quad \lambda = \frac{w_{ij} - w^{(\ell)}}{w^{(\ell+1)} - w^{(\ell)}}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Linearisation of the CII Constraint}
\label{app:notation:linearisation}

The attained \CII{} constraint requires:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\sum_{(i,j)} \sum_v x_{ij,v} \cdot \mathrm{CO}_{2,ij,v} \cdot 10^6}{C_{\mathrm{eff}} \cdot D_T} \leq \ciithr.
  \label{eq:cii_constraint_app}
\end{equation}

Since $D_T$ is constant (the set of arcs and their distances do not depend
on speed choices), multiplying both sides by $C_{\mathrm{eff}} \cdot D_T > 0$
preserves the inequality direction and yields the linear constraint:
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{(i,j) \in \setA} \sum_{v \in \setV} x_{ij,v} \cdot \mathrm{CO}_{2,ij,v}
  \leq \ciithr \cdot C_{\mathrm{eff}} \cdot D_T \cdot 10^{-6},
  \label{eq:cii_linear_app}
\end{equation}
which is linear in the binary decision variables $x_{ij,v}$.  No
auxiliary variables or big-M reformulations are required.
